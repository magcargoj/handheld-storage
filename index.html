How to Choose the Right Storage

Steam Deck: LCD vs. OLED

The Steam Deck supports UHS-I MicroSD cards. While you can insert a faster UHS-II card, the Deck's slot is capped at UHS-I speeds (~100MB/s), so you won't see a performance benefit. For internal upgrades, the Steam Deck requires a specific M.2 2230 SSD . Do not buy a standard 2280 PC drive; it will not fit.

The Nintendo Switch 2 uses the MicroSD Express standard to handle next-gen textures and fast loading times . Standard UHS-I cards are NOT recommended. Even for backward-compatible Switch 1 games, the system architecture relies on the high-speed Express lanes.

PC Handhelds (Legion, ROG, MSI)

The market is split on storage standards. The ROG Ally and Legion Go support high-speed UHS-II SD cards (300MB/s+), which are great for running AAA games externally. However, internal SSD sizes vary wildly:

  • Steam Deck / ROG Ally: Uses M.2 2230 (Small)
  • Legion Go / Go S: Uses M.2 2242 (Medium)
  • Legion 2 / Ally X: Uses M.2 2280 (Standard PC Size)

Retro Handhelds (AYN, Anbernic, Powkiddy)

For devices like the AYN Odin 2, Retroid Pocket 4, or Anbernic RG35XX, speed matters less than capacity. These devices generally do not support UHS-II or Express speeds. A high-capacity, reliable UHS-I card (like a Samsung EVO or SanDisk Ultra) is the most cost-effective way to store thousands of ROMs.
